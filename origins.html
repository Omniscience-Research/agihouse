Origins of AGI House.

AGI House.

The backstory of our beloved machine learning hacker houses.

From NeoGenesis to AGI House.
By Jeremy Nixon NeoGenesis Lease Future Forum

I founded Neogenesis with Andrej Karpathy in February, 2021, where we held epic out-of-this-world future tech events like Future Forum.
Marco Mascorro, Andrej Karpathy and I started hosting hackathons with fantastic technologists, like the creators of Stable Diffusion. The hackathons were so productive that in September 2022, Marco and I had long conversations about a new creative idea - we could rename NeoGenesis to AGI House and mainly run AI focused events.

I had 4 house managers at NeoGenesis. The house manager's job is to help collect rent and help with events. First Ashley Zhang, then Daniela Velez, then Rebecca Chen, and then Rocky Yu. After running AGI House hackathons for ~3 months, Tim told me that Rocky wanted to take on more responsibility & try to raise a fund. This sounded great to me, because running events is time consuming and I wanted to spend more time building my company (we just launched!). I decided to bring Rocky on {photo of my dated to-do list}, and he and Tim took on the next lease.
In May, I started to think about creating an SF branch of AGI House. We found a glorious space and have started running even more incredible events.
The unifying element between the two houses is that they share the same founding vision and the same person created both of them.
We've run multiple ~100 person events out of the AGI House slack in the past few weeks and will continue to do that. In the last 9 days, everything from our CodeGen hackathon w/ Amjad Masad (CEO of Replit) to our Launch Your own Large Language Model hackathon w/ Modal & Google, to our SF acceleration meetup w/ Garry Tan. Our hackathon with the creators of Stable Diffusion is coming up, and we’re going to run glorious hackathons all year long, so do keep in touch!
