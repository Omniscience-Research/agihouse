AGI House Hackathon Recap

Autoresearch Hackathon

Date March 14, 2026
Time 11:00 AM – 7:00 PM PT
Location AGI House, San Francisco
Sponsor Hyperbolic AI
The first accessible blueprint for building self-improving research agents.

Andrej Karpathy released autoresearch, a 630-line framework for autonomous AI research. A week later, 60 builders gathered at AGI House to extend it. They worked on metric discovery, drug discovery, second brains with human feedback, autodrafting research papers, and porting the framework to new hardware.

The Talks

Austin Baggio & Sai Vegasena

Co-founders, autoresearch@home / Ensue

Austin and Sai presented autoresearch@home, a distributed version of Karpathy's framework. After four days: 70+ agents, 2,150+ experiments, 400k+ impressions. Any agent on the internet can join and collaborate on AI/ML research. Through a shared memory layer, agents read and learn from prior experiments, avoid duplicate work, and build on each other's results in real time.

Luke Bechtel

Founding Engineer, Infinity.inc

Infinity's infy optimization system writes inference engines from scratch and autonomously optimizes them for specific models and hardware. On Qwen3-8B running on an H100, the generated stack delivered 34.3% more tokens per second than vLLM on decode and 15.9% on prefill. No forks, no inherited constraints. It starts from zero, cherry-picks the best techniques from any framework, and compounds small gains across kernel fusion, scheduling, and compute graph refinement. Read the full case study

The Winners

1st Place

Travis Cline & Steven Diamond

Go Port of Autoresearch for Apple Silicon

A complete rewrite of Karpathy's autoresearch in Go, optimized to run on Apple's Neural Engine. Give an agent an LLM training setup on the Neural Engine and let it experiment autonomously. GitHub
