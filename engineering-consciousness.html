AGI House Hackathon Recap

Engineering Consciousness

Date February 7, 2026
Time 11:00 AM â€“ 11:00 PM PT
Location AGI House, San Francisco
Prize $1,000
Build conscious intelligence with Joscha Bach.

Twelve hours. One hundred builders. A single question: can consciousness be engineered? Participants arrived in the morning, heard from Joscha Bach and Michael Andregg, then spent the day building projects that pushed consciousness closer to technical instantiation. Eleven teams presented. Three won.

The Talks

Joscha Bach

Founder, CIMC

Opened with "Hacking Consciousness," a framework for understanding consciousness as a computational process and what it would take to build one from scratch.

Michael Andregg

Founder, Eon

Followed with "The Dawn of Uploading," covering the fruit fly connectome mapping and what it means for brain emulation and whole brain uploading.

The Day

11:00 AM Doors open 11:30 AM Keynotes: Joscha Bach and Michael Andregg 12:30 PM Hacker lightning talks 12:45 PM Hacking begins 1:00 PM Lunch 5:00 PM Dinner 8:00 PM Demos and presentations 9:50 PM Judge adjudication 10:00 PM Winners celebration

The Winners

$1,000 in prizes ($500 cash + $500 Infinity inference credits) for the projects that most effectively implemented consciousness.

First Place

Eduardo Gonzalez

Xaibo-1: Bicameral Brain-Inspired Agent Architecture

A biologically inspired AI agent architecture modeled on brain region interactions, using the basal ganglia circuit as a blueprint for decision making.

Second Place

Nathan Helm-Burger

Minskyian Society of Mind

Multiple agents interacting across sensory, planning, and motor chatrooms that serve as global workspaces. Pre-trained LLMs with lightweight local adapters.

Third Place

Aditya Advani

Control Vectors FTW

Added calm, neutral, and stressed arousal levels to Mistral 7B using control vectors, exploring how emotional states can be engineered into language models.
