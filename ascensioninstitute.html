Coordinating capital toward the frontiers of research automation

The people building the most transformative tools in history are uniquely positioned to direct where they are applied. We exist to make that coordination happen.

The Observation

The researchers automating science are the only ones who can steer it

Engineers at the frontier AI labs sit at an unprecedented intersection: they possess both deep technical knowledge of what these systems can do, and increasingly, the financial capital to act on that knowledge.

Meanwhile, the researchers best positioned to identify which problems matter most — the scientists pushing at the boundaries of biology, physics, mathematics — have the expertise but lack the resources. Many face resistance within their own institutions, fighting to adopt the very tools that could transform their fields.

These two groups need each other. No one else is building the bridge.

The Gap

Academia cannot lead this transition alone

Traditional institutions were designed for a different pace of discovery. Tenure incentives reward incrementalism. Funding cycles are measured in years, not the weeks it now takes for a capability to emerge. The researchers who see what is coming are working against the academic zeitgeist.

The question is not whether research will be automated. It is how deliberately we choose which systems to automate, and who is making those decisions.

“How do we think more deliberately about which systems we choose to automate — and ensure the people closest to the frontier are the ones directing it?”

The Approach

A new model for coordinating research capital

Ascension Institute directs intellectual and financial capital toward concrete problems at the frontier of automated research. We identify the scientists leading the adoption of AI tools within their fields, connect them with the technologists and capital that can accelerate their work, and create the structures for sustained collaboration.

I

Identify

Find the researchers operating at the edge — those who understand what AI can do for their discipline and are pushing forward despite institutional resistance. These are the scientists whose work will define the next decade of discovery.

II

Connect

Bring together frontier AI engineers — the people with both technical depth and capital — with the researchers who know which problems are worth solving. Create the conditions for genuine intellectual exchange, not transactional networking.

III

Direct

Coordinate the flow of capital toward specific, high-leverage research automation efforts. Not broad mandates, but targeted investments in the tools and systems that will compress decades of scientific progress into years.

The Landscape

Two groups, one missing link

Frontier Engineers

Knowledge of what AI systems can do. Growing financial capital. Awareness of which capabilities are emerging next. Looking for meaningful ways to deploy both.

Research Scientists

Domain expertise across critical fields. Understanding of which problems matter. Fighting institutional inertia to adopt new tools. In need of capital and collaborators who understand the technology.

Ascension exists at the intersection. We are the coordination layer — identifying the highest-leverage opportunities to accelerate scientific progress through the deliberate application of AI to the research process itself.
